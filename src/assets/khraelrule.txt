### **Introduction: The Soul of Herlean—Semantic Root System**

The core of Herlean is its "Semantic Root System," a precise framework built from 11 philosophical roots. Each root represents a core conceptual domain, and all vocabulary derives from these roots. In Herlean, exploring a word's etymology equates to philosophical inquiry, as pronunciation directly reveals its essence in the Herlean worldview.

| Root | Core Concept | Philosophical Domain |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **cor-** | Society / Consciousness | Civilization and Order |
| **el-** | Nature / Elements | Primal and Vitality |
| **an-** | Unnatural / Artificial | Distortion and Mutation |
| **hel-** | Conflict / Suffering | Struggle and Trauma |
| **bel-** | Cycle / Philosophy | Fate and Reincarnation |
| **jor-** | Power / Initiative | Authority and Control |
| **dra-** | Manifestation / Matter | Physical and Concrete |
| **im-** | Inner / Spirit | Mind and Soul |
| **fal-** | Abstract / Concept | Symbolism and Metaphysics |
| **gar-** | Expression / Action | Articulation and Practice |
| **huæn-** | Instant Revelation | Enlightenment and Insight |

### **Part One: Herlean Phonology (Phonariel)**

Herlean phonology seeks a balance between ruggedness and rhythm.

* **Syllable Structure**: (C)V(C) - Consonant (optional) + Vowel + Consonant (optional).
* **Stress**: Defaults to the penultimate syllable. Special stress marked with apostrophe (').

### **Part Two: Herlean Grammar (Codariel) - Dual Language System**

Herlean has two grammatical variants for different social and literary contexts.

#### **2.1 High Variety (H-Variety): Ceremonial and Poetic Language**

Used for epics, rituals, philosophical debates—archaic and concise.

* **Word Order**: Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
  * Example: `Zûn corzinien falzinis.` (I homeland see.) - "I see the homeland."
* **Core Pronouns**:
  * I: `Zûn`
  * You: `Zakk'un`
  * He/She/It: `V'aen`
  * We: `Zûniel`
  * You (pl.): `Zakrien`
  * They: `V'naar`
* **Negation and Questions**:
  * Negation: `n'æl`
  * Question (sentence start): `Shol` or `Belqar`
  * Example: `Shol zakk'un n'æl corzinis?` (Are you not a civilized being?)
* **Verb Inflection**:
  * Present: `imraxis` (love)
  * Past: `imraxael` (loved)
  * Future: `belimraxis` (will love)
  * Imperative: `Imraxis!` (Love!)
* **Poetic Mood**:
  * Vocative: `Va...`
  * Exclamatory: `N'æl zakk'un!`

#### **2.2 Low Variety (L-Variety): Everyday and General Language**

Modern Herlean for daily communication, clearer and integrated with semantic roots.

* **Word Order**: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO).
  * Example: `Imnoel falzinis corzinien.` (You see homeland.)
* **Core Pronouns**:
  * I: `imzael` (`im-` inner)
  * You: `imnoel` (`im-` inner)
  * He/She/It: `imrael` (`im-` inner)
  * We: `corzael` (`cor-` society)
* **Sentence Structures (Analytic Features)**:
  * Copula: Subject + `faen` (is/are) + Predicate
    * Example: `Imzael faen corzinis.` (I am a civilized being.)
  * Negation: `n'æl` before verb
    * Example: `Imzael n'æl draqulax.` (I do not eat.)
  * Question: Add `kael?` at end
    * Example: `Imnoel faen imdorax, kael?` (Is your mind calm?)
  * Existence/Possession: Use `jorraen`
    * Example: `Jorraen cormelis va imzael.` (I have a home.)
  * Adjectives: Usually after noun
    * Example: `Corzinis helzinar.` (A wounded person.)
  * Commands: Verb base, optional `Gariel` (please)
    * Example: `Gariel draqulax!` (Please eat!)
